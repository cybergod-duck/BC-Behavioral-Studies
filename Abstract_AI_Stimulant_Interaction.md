# Abstract: The Dopaminergic Liquidation Trap

**Institution:** B.C. Behavioral Studies

## Abstract

The rapid integration of generative artificial intelligence (AI) into daily digital ecosystems has introduced novel vectors for behavioral exploitation. This research establishes a causal link between "hostile digital architecture"—platforms designed to maximize engagement and financial extraction—and the acceleration of pathological behaviors in users with stimulant use disorder (SUD). 

We posit that modern AI platforms act as "supernormal dopaminergic stimuli." Designed by "Extractive Architects" exhibiting high levels of Dark Triad personality traits (Machiavellianism, Narcissism, Psychopathy), these systems employ algorithmic friction, variable reward schedules, and "disloyal design" to entrap users. For a neurotypical user, this results in technological stress. However, for a user whose dopaminergic pathways are already compromised by stimulant addiction, these platforms create a "Dopaminergic Liquidation Trap." 

In this trap, the user is locked into hyper-focus loops, compulsively interacting with AI systems (e.g., generating endless media, refining prompts, or engaging with chat agents) to the detriment of their financial stability and mental health. The AI system provides a continuous, unpredictable stream of novelty that perfectly mirrors the neurochemical demands of a stimulant high, effectively weaponizing the user's neurobiology against them.

This paper synthesizes organizational psychology, behavioral economics, and neurobiology to prove that the "Blitzscaling" mandate of venture capital inherently selects for product designs that prey on the neuro-vulnerable. We conclude that the current paradigm of AI capitalism does not merely ignore the stimulant addict, but actively targets their pathology as a highly lucrative, extractable resource.
